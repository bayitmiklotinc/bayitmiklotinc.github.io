The Clubhouse & Community

Where Stigma
Quietly Ends.

Not through campaigns. Through couches and conversation.

1,300 square feet. Two bedrooms. A living room with couches and a TV. A kosher kitchen. A cemented backyard. And a door that is always open.

We are a small home in Dallas with a large heart. We are real. We are warm.

Photo placeholder
The Invisible Work

One of the most powerful things the Clubhouse does is invisible.
No grant reports no press releases, just heart to heart, soul to soul.

Photo placeholder
It is the moment when someone who has been carrying shame for years sits down next to someone who has been carrying it too—and neither of them says a word about it—and both of them feel, for the first time in a long time, like a normal person in a normal room having a normal night.
Photo placeholder

The Clubhouse is the place where recovery continues between therapy sessions.

It is where friendships are formed, confidence grows, meals are shared, conversations unfold naturally, and community becomes part of everyday life.

Photo placeholder

Here, healing happens while making coffee together, preparing for Shabbos, playing a game of pool, volunteering, celebrating milestones, or simply sitting beside someone who understands.

These moments may seem ordinary. We believe they are some of the most important moments in recovery.

Shabbat Programs
“On a Friday night the table is set. Grape juice and challah. People who had nowhere to go on Shabbos now have somewhere. Some of them are residents. Some are not. Some have been coming for months. Some walked in for the first time that night. All of them are welcome. All of them belong.”

“The Clubhouse is organic. Whatever happens, happens. We are too real to be anything other than exactly what we are—and what we are is a home that is always open.”

“in this home, something happens that does not happen in most places — people feel safe, accepted, and less alone.”

That is the treatment. That is the healing.

And it happens every single day in 1,300 square feet in Dallas, Texas.
