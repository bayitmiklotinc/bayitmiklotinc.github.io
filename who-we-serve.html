Who We Serve

Every Person Deserves a Place
to Belong, Heal, and Rebuild.

At Bayit Miklot, we support individuals and families facing a wide range of life challenges. The areas below represent many of the people we serve, but they are not exhaustive. Every person's journey is unique, and we thoughtfully consider each situation to determine how Bayit Miklot is the right environment to provide meaningful support.

Photo placeholder

Addiction

Recovery is about far more than stopping a behavior. We support individuals seeking lasting healing through housing, clinical care, community, meaningful purpose, and genuine human connection.

Behavioral Addiction

Not every addiction involves a substance. Compulsive behaviors such as gambling, pornography, gaming, technology use, shopping, or other repetitive patterns can be just as disruptive and isolating. We recognize these struggles with the same compassion and respect, helping individuals rebuild healthy lives through connection, accountability, and personalized support.

Mental Health

For those living with anxiety, depression, bipolar disorder, psychosis, or other mental health challenges, Bayit Miklot provides a safe and structured environment where recovery is approached with dignity, compassion, and individualized support.

Trauma

Many struggles begin long before addiction or crisis. We recognize the lasting impact of trauma and create an environment where healing happens at a pace that respects each person's story.

Homelessness & Housing Instability

Some people don't need another program—they need a safe home where they can stabilize, rebuild, and reconnect. Bayit Miklot provides that foundation from which recovery can begin.

Families

Addiction and mental health challenges affect entire families. We believe families deserve support, education, hope, and partnership throughout the recovery journey whenever appropriate.

Alumni

Recovery doesn't end when someone leaves our residence. Through the Bayit Miklot Clubhouse, mentorship, shared experiences, and lifelong community, alumni remain part of the Bayit Miklot family long after they transition into independent living.

And More

The people we serve cannot always be defined by a diagnosis or a circumstance. If you or someone you care about is facing challenges not listed here, we encourage you to reach out.

Every inquiry is considered thoughtfully and individually because our focus is on the whole person—not just the problem they are facing.
