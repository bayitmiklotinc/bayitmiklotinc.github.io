Our Founder

Meet Avi Herman

Recovery did more than save my life—it gave me responsibility and purpose.

Photo placeholder

I was born in Brooklyn in 1992. From the age of 9 and for more than twenty years, I lived under the grip of addiction. Everything changed when I reached the point where I finally knew I could no longer do it alone. I finally asked for help!

Shalom. My name is Avi Herman. Today I am grateful to be more than 1,000 days in recovery.

As I started to rebuild my own life, I continued meeting young Jewish men and women facing the very same battles I had fought; addiction, mental illness, trauma, homelessness, and profound isolation. Again and again, I saw people who wanted to recover but had nowhere safe to live, nowhere to belong, and no community prepared to walk beside them after treatment ended.

That realization became Bayit Miklot.

With little more than faith, determination, and a single home in Dallas, I opened my door to those who had nowhere else to go. What began with one house and a handful of residents has grown into a community built on dignity, accountability, compassion, and hope.

During my period of addiction, I experienced overdoses, psychiatric hospitalizations, countless rehabilitation programs, destructive relationships, and the overwhelming feeling that I no longer belonged anywhere—not even within my own community.

There were moments when life felt completely without purpose and I believed I was beyond help. Everything changed for me in 2023, when I reached the point where I could no longer do it alone. I asked for help.
Photo placeholder

My work with Bayit Miklot is deeply personal. Some days I am meeting with families, helping residents navigate recovery, or speaking with donors. Other days I am cleaning the kitchen late at night or answering a phone call at three in the morning from someone who simply needs to know they are not alone.

The need is great and very urgent. and it is very real.

I founded Bayit Miklot because I reached a point where I could no longer watch people suffer without doing something. Too many lives have been lost. Too many families have been left believing there was nowhere to turn. Bayit Miklot exists to change that.

“Recovery does not happen just between office hours. I answer the three in the morning calls myself—because that is when people need to know they are not alone.”
Avi Herman
Founder & CEO
